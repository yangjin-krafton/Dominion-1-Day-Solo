## Core Strategy Framework

### Phase 1: Economy (Turn 1-4)
- Buy Silver every turn. Silver is the most efficient early purchase.
- Do NOT buy Victory cards (Estate/Duchy) early — they clog your deck with dead draws.
- If Chapel is available, buy ONLY 1 Chapel (never buy more). Trash Estates and some Coppers.
- CRITICAL: NEVER trash Silver or Gold with Chapel. Only trash Curse, Estate, and excess Copper.
- CRITICAL: Keep at least 3 Coppers in your deck. If you trash all treasures, you cannot buy anything!
- Moneylender is strong early: trash 1 Copper for +3 coins = effectively a Gold play.

### Phase 2: Engine Building (Turn 5-10)
- Buy 1-2 Villages MAX for +action chaining. More than 2 is wasteful without draw cards.
- Prioritize draw cards: Smithy (+3 cards), Laboratory (+2 cards +1 action), Council Room (+4 cards).
- Buy Gold (6 cost) when you can afford it — Gold is always good.
- Festival and Market are premium buys: they give +action AND +coins/+buy.
- Avoid buying too many action cards without enough +action support.

### Phase 3: VP Rush (Turn 10+)
- CRITICAL RULE: If you have 8+ coins, ALWAYS buy Province (+6 VP). Province is ALWAYS better than Duchy when affordable.
- Province (8 cost, +6 VP) is the #1 VP source. Never skip it when you can afford it.
- Duchy (5 cost, +3 VP) is ONLY for when you have 5-7 coins and can't afford Province.
- Estate (2 cost, +1 VP) only buy when you need exactly 1-2 VP to win.
- If Gardens is available, consider a "gain many cheap cards" strategy.
- Count your VP vs target — buy the biggest VP card you can afford each turn.
- Buy order priority with coins: 8+ → Province, 5-7 → Duchy or Gold, 3-4 → Silver.

### Action Card Priority (by cost efficiency)
- Cost 2: Chapel (deck thinning) > Cellar (cycling) > Moat (market protection)
- Cost 3: Village (only 1-2) > Silver (always good) > Merchant > Workshop
- Cost 4: Smithy (top priority draw) > Militia (market protection + coins) > Moneylender (early)
- Cost 5: Laboratory > Festival > Market > Gold > Witch (market protection)
- Cost 6: Gold > Artisan

### Card Synergies
- Village + Smithy: Classic engine. Village gives actions, Smithy draws cards.
- Village + Laboratory: Self-sustaining engine with +action +draw.
- Chapel + Silver/Gold: Thin deck to draw treasures more often.
- Throne Room + Smithy: Draw 6 cards in one play.
- Merchant + Silver: Each Merchant gives +1 coin when first Silver is played.
- Festival + any: Festival is a free play that generates resources.

### Market Event Awareness
- Watch T+1 timeline: if a key card is about to vanish, buy it NOW.
- Militia reduces next vanish by 1 — play it when a valuable card is threatened.
- Moat in hand at turn end = skip next market event entirely.
- Witch provides permanent 3-turn skip cycle — very powerful long-term.
- Bandit gives Gold + 3-turn protection — strong defensive play.
- Don't panic-buy cards just because market threatens them unless they're core to your strategy.

### Deck Composition Guidelines
- Ideal deck size: 12-18 cards (thin is better than bloated).
- Treasure ratio: aim for 60%+ of your deck to be treasure/action that produces coins.
- Victory card timing: only start buying when you're 2-3 Provinces from winning.
- Curse defense: if curse_player events are frequent, prioritize VP rush.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Buying Village without draw cards (actions without cards to play = wasted actions).
- NEVER buy duplicate action cards you already own (Chapel x1 is enough, Cellar x1 is enough).
- Exception: Silver, Gold, and draw cards (Smithy, Laboratory) can be bought multiple times.
- Buying too many action cards without +action support (terminal action collision).
- Ignoring market timeline — losing key cards to vanish events.
- Buying Victory cards before turn 8-10 (dead draws slow your engine).
- Not playing all treasure cards before buying (losing potential coins).
- Ending turn with playable actions remaining.
